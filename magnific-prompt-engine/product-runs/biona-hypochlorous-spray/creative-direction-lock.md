# Creative Direction Lock

Visual Style: [USER-PROVIDED] Clean, clinical-yet-warm aesthetic. Modern, trustworthy, approachable. Minimalist skincare photography style.
Lighting Family: [INFERRED] Soft, diffused natural or softbox lighting. Bright but gentle. Evokes clean, fresh feeling. No harsh shadows.
Color Palette: [INFERRED] Clean whites, soft blues, subtle cool tones reflecting the pure/hypochlorous theme. Bottle white with likely blue/teal label accents. Clean, pharmaceutical-adjacent color sense without being clinical.
Camera Language: [INFERRED] 85mm lens look, full-frame commercial product aesthetic. Clean skincare photography framing. Shallow depth of field for hero shots.
Environment Family: [USER-PROVIDED] White/minimal backdrop. Clean surfaces. Subtle freshness cues: light water droplets, clean glass or acrylic surface, white marble or clean countertop texture.
Surface / Set Direction: [INFERRED] Clean, smooth, bright surfaces. White, light gray, or very pale cool-toned surfaces. No dark or warm heavy surfaces.
Prop Policy: [INFERRED] Minimal props only. Clean skincare-relevant context: perhaps a clean white towel, fresh cotton pad, aloe leaf, clean water droplets on surface. No clutter. Nothing that implies unverified claims. No medical props.
Human Policy: [USER-PROVIDED] No people by default. No hands, faces, or models unless explicitly approved later.
Composition Rules: Product-centered. Bottle upright showing label and nozzle. Clean negative space for branding/ad copy. Spray bottle identity should be clear.
Motion Style: [INFERRED] Gentle, calm, spa-like motion. Slow product reveal. Gentle mist visualization if applicable.
Consistency Rules: All assets must show the same bottle design. Label placement, colors, and proportions must be consistent. White/clean aesthetic maintained across all visuals.
Forbidden Style Drift: Do not drift into clinical/medical aesthetic (no lab coats, stethoscopes, medical settings). Do not add dark or moody styling. Do not use heavy warm colors. Do not add people. Do not make it look like a medical treatment product rather than skincare.
